# Phase 2: Strategic Brief

Using intelligence_report.md, build a strategic brief that will guide carousel creation.

## Step 2.1 — Viral Angle Selection

Identify the **single highest-opportunity content angle** — the intersection of:

1. **Audience demand** — What does the target audience most want to learn?
2. **Competitor gaps** — What are competitors NOT saying well enough?
3. **Current traction** — What is gaining momentum right now?

### Selection Framework

**Score each potential angle (1-10):**

| Angle | Audience Demand | Competitor Gap | Current Traction | Total Score |
|-------|----------------|----------------|------------------|-------------|
| "5 myths about X" | 8 | 6 | 7 | 21 |
| "How to Y in Z days" | 9 | 4 | 8 | 21 |
| "Stop doing A, do B" | 7 | 9 | 6 | 22 |

**Select the highest-scoring angle.**

### Document the Chosen Angle

```markdown
## Chosen Angle: [Title]

**Why this angle works:**

- **Audience demand (score/10):** [Evidence from intelligence_report.md]
- **Competitor gap (score/10):** [Evidence from competitor audit]
- **Current traction (score/10):** [Evidence from trend intelligence]

**Value proposition:** This carousel will [specific outcome] by [unique approach]

**Authority signal:** [Why this business is credible on this topic]
```

## Step 2.2 — Hook Formula

Write **3 alternative Slide 1 hooks** using proven formulas.

### Hook A: Bold Contrarian Claim

Challenge common wisdom or reveal an uncomfortable truth.

**Formula:** "[Widely accepted belief] is costing you [specific loss]. Here's what actually works."

**Example:** "Instagram engagement pods are killing your reach. Here's the algorithm hack nobody talks about."

**Emotional trigger:** Surprise + validation (they suspected something was wrong)

### Hook B: Specific Outcome Promise

Promise a clear, desirable result with specificity.

**Formula:** "How to [achieve specific outcome] in [specific timeframe] using [specific method]"

**Example:** "How to get 10K Instagram followers in 90 days using carousels (even if you hate showing your face)"

**Emotional trigger:** Aspiration + feasibility

### Hook C: Pattern Interrupt Question

Ask a question that triggers instant curiosity or self-reflection.

**Formula:** "[Provocative question that challenges their current approach]"

**Example:** "What if I told you your Instagram strategy is backwards?"

**Emotional trigger:** Curiosity + cognitive dissonance

### Select the Strongest Hook

- **Scroll-stopping power:** [Why this stops mid-scroll]
- **Thumbnail legibility:** [How it reads at 200px size]
- **Audience alignment:** [Why this resonates with the target audience]
- **Differentiation:** [How this stands out from competitors]

## Step 2.3 — Slide-by-Slide Content Structure

Design an **8–10 slide structure** optimized for saves and shares.

### Recommended Structure

**Slide 1: Hook**
- Use the chosen hook from Step 2.2
- Make it visually bold and instantly readable
- Thumbnail test: legible at 200x200px

**Slides 2–7: Value Delivery**
- One insight per slide (avoid cramming multiple points)
- Build logically (each slide sets up the next)
- Include authority signals (data, examples, credentials)

**Slide 8: Key Takeaway**
- Most saveable slide — make it reference-worthy
- Formats that get saved:
  - Checklist
  - Framework diagram
  - Before/After comparison
  - Quick-reference matrix

**Slide 9: CTA**
- One clear action (follow, save, share, comment, DM)
- Warm, conversational tone
- No hard selling — value-first

**Slide 10 (optional): Brand/Offer**
- Only if there's a relevant offer or service
- Soft pitch — position as next logical step
- Include clear way to take action

### Content Template for Each Slide

For **Slides 2–7**, structure each as:

```markdown
### Slide [N]: [Slide Title]

**Headline copy:** [8 words or fewer, bold statement]

**Body copy:** [1-2 lines, max 20 words, supports the headline]

**Authority signal:** [Data point, credential, or example that proves credibility]

**Visual direction:** [Icon, image, or graphic element to include]
```

### Example Slide Structure

```markdown
### Slide 2: The Algorithm Changed in 2024

**Headline:** Instagram now prioritizes "sends per reach"

**Body:** If people share your content privately, you win.

**Authority signal:** Instagram head Adam Mosseri confirmed this in Dec 2024.

**Visual direction:** Screenshot of Mosseri's statement + arrow pointing to "sends" metric
```
