# QA Rubric

Use this checklist before a Notion deliverable is sent or published.

## Scorecard

Score each category from 0 to 3.

### 1. First Viewport Clarity

- 0: The page gives no clear orientation.
- 1: The purpose is visible, but next action is unclear.
- 2: Purpose, audience, and next action are clear.
- 3: The top viewport feels polished, intentional, and immediately useful.

### 2. Navigation And Architecture

- 0: Flat or chaotic page.
- 1: Some headings exist, but scanning is hard.
- 2: Clear sections, TOC/navigation where useful.
- 3: Strong hub-and-spoke structure with clean pages/views.

### 3. Visual Hierarchy

- 0: Wall of text or visual clutter.
- 1: Basic headings only.
- 2: Good use of headings, callouts, dividers, columns, and media.
- 3: Restrained premium feel with strong rhythm and no competing decoration.

### 4. Database/View Usefulness

- 0: No useful structure or database is unnecessary.
- 1: Database exists but views/properties are messy.
- 2: Properties and views support real workflows.
- 3: Clean database product with templates, filters, sorts, and role-specific
  views.

### 5. Content Polish

- 0: Raw notes, rambling, or obvious placeholders.
- 1: Useful but verbose or uneven.
- 2: Clear, concise, and mostly final.
- 3: Client-ready or public-ready prose with no filler.

### 6. Public/Client Safety

- 0: Exposes secrets or internal/private work.
- 1: Some questionable internal material remains.
- 2: No obvious private exposure.
- 3: Explicitly checked for secrets, internal scratch notes, and subpage leakage.

### 7. Publishing Readiness

- 0: Not publishable.
- 1: Missing media, broken links, or internal URL only.
- 2: Mostly ready, minor issues fixed.
- 3: Published URL verified, images/links checked, final link ready.

## Passing Thresholds

- Internal draft: 14/21
- Internal operating system: 16/21
- Client-facing page: 18/21
- Public Notion Site: 19/21

## Failure Rules

Revise before sending if any of these are true:

- Public/client safety is below 2.
- Publishing readiness is below 2 for any page expected to be sent externally.
- The page has no clear first-viewport orientation.
- A database has more properties than useful views.
- The page contains visible placeholder/test artifacts such as "Placeholder",
  "Test icon fix", "TODO", "TBD", "insert screenshot", "lorem ipsum", or
  "replace this".
- The page has obvious visual scaffolding glitches, repeated orphan symbols, or
  empty decorative elements that make it look unfinished.
- The agent would need to apologize for how the page looks.
